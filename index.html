External USB Charger

I've gone through a few external chargers. Mostly cheap mini chargers that barely worked or made me nervous I was damaging my phone. One even smelled strange when using. Not good.

So, when I found the Anker Astro E3 Portable Charger was $21 I thought I'd give it a go. It's not their newest version hence the cost. It's not the slimmest or lightest but it charges my phone 4 times before I have to plug it in overnight. Has 2 ports so limits fighting between devices. I didn't expect to love it as much as I have.

My current test showed it charging my Iphone6 at 15% to 100% in 1.45hrs. I left the phone on because that's how I normally use/charge my phone.

Go Get It via Amazon

External USB Solar Charger

I've experimented with a few solar chargers - most were either too heavy, didn't charge/recharge fast enough, or both.

The Bushnell SolarWrap Mini works, is light and small. I take this on road trips, lay it on the car dashboard or wherever and let it recharge. The roll up feature is nice and compact. I charge it when I remember to but mostly just bring it outside and it does the job.

Go Get It via Bushnell

Wireless Keyboard

Wireless keyboards are great for a few reasons. One, they can help add flexibility to your work station. Two, makes it super easy to take that station with you. Third, allows you to work from a variety of monitor types (tablet, laptop, phone, etc).

The Apple Wireless Keyboard is the best I've seen that satisfy all of my mobile requirements. It's super portable, light, and uses 2 AA batteries. I do wish it had an internal rechargable option built in. Sometimes the bluetooth gets buggy for me but overall a solid performance.

Go Get It via Apple
